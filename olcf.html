The Oak Ridge Leadership Computing Facility (OLCF) was established at Oak Ridge National Laboratory in 2004 with the mission of standing up a supercomputer 100 times more powerful than the leading systems of the day.

The Technology Integration group was instrumental in designing and implementing the infrastructure required to deliver Jaguar four years later, in 2008. TechInt...

  • designed the Lustre-based "Spider" file system.
  • improved Lustre so it would meet the Center's needs through feedback to the developers as well as by developing and submitting patches.
  • developed intelligent network routing strategies.
  • contributed to the design, development, deployment, and ongoing maintenance of the High Performance Storage System (HPSS), the OLCF's archival storage solution.

Current Technology Integration projects in support of the OLCF mission include projects to identify optimal strategies for allocating resources on multi-core systems, improve I/O performance by coordinating transfers so they don't interfere with each other, understand how non-volatile memory can be used to enhance computational performance, design and develop a generic high performance network communication interface to simplify network programming, and maintain and enhance High Performance Storage System (HPSS).

Looking forward, TechInt is working to identify, refine, and deploy the storage and networking solutions required to operate increasingly fast supercomputers, from 20 petaflops in 2012 to an exaflop by the end of the decade (2018).

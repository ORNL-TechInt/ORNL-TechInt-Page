Sudharshan Vazhkudai

Sudharshan is the Group Leader of the Technology Integration group in the National Center for Computational Sciences (NCCS) at Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL). NCCS hosts the multi-petaflop Titan supercomputer.

Prior to that, Sudharshan was a Research Scientist in the Computer Science Research group of the Computer Science and Mathematics Division at ORNL, for over nine years. He joined ORNL in August 2003. In this role, his work had straddled both basic and applied distributed systems research. On the applied side of things, he worked on building a distributed computing/data management solution for the DOE's Spallation Neutron Source. From a basic research standpoint, his focus has been on HPC I/O, storage systems and multicore architectures.

Sudharshan received a Ph.D. and Masters in Computer Science from the University of Mississippi in 2003 and 1998 respectively. His doctoral work (partly at Argonne National Laboratory, in Chicago, on a Wallace Givens Fellowship and a Dissertation Fellowship) addressed data access issues in the Globus Data Grid environment. In what appears to be a previous life (if you believe in that sort of a thing :)), Sudharshan obtained a bachelors degree in computer science from Karnatak University in India in 1996. He grew up in Madras, South India.

Sudharshan Vazhkudai

I am the Group Leader of the Technology Integration group in the National Center for Computational Sciences (NCCS) at Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL). NCCS hosts the multi-petaflop Titan supercomputer.

Prior to that, I was a Research Scientist in the Computer Science Research group of the Computer Science and Mathematics Division at ORNL, for over nine years. I joined ORNL in August 2003. In this role, my work had straddled both basic and applied distributed systems research. On the applied side of things, I worked on building a distributed computing/data management solution for the DOE's Spallation Neutron Source. From a basic research standpoint, my focus has been on HPC I/O, storage systems and multicore architectures.

I received a Ph.D. and Masters in Computer Science from the University of Mississippi in 2003 and 1998 respectively. My doctoral work (partly at Argonne National Laboratory, in Chicago, where I was on a Wallace Givens Fellowship and a Dissertation Fellowship) addressed data access issues in the Globus Data Grid environment. In what appears to be my previous life (if you believe in that sort of a thing :)), obtained a bachelors degree in computer science from Karnatak University in India in 1996. I grew up in Madras, South India.
